Tales Of Biodiversity Of The Western Ghats

  • Vibinraj kk
  • Travel
  • 14k

Geography and Location:The Western Ghats hills is a continuos mountain range that runs parallel to western coast of india.In kerala,the western ghats cover a significant portion of the eastern side of the state,stretching from the northern district of kasargod to the southern district of Thiruvananthapuram.

Biodiversity Hotspot:The western Ghats is globally recognized as one of the world's eight hotspot of biological diversity.This mountain range is home to a vast array of plant and animals species,including many endemic and endangered ones.The dense forest,grasslands,rivers and waterfalls of the western ghats in kerala support an incredible diversity of flora and funa.

Protected areas:Several national parks,wildlife sancturies and biospheres reserves have been established in western ghats of kerala to conserve its unique ecosystem.some notable areas include silent valley national park ,periyar tiger reserves,eravikulam national park,wayanad wild life sanctuary and parambikulam wild life sanctuary.

The western Ghats Hills Plays a Cruicial Role in Maintaining The Ecological Balance of Kerala.

Endemic Species:The wetsern ghats hills in kerala are home of numeruos species that are found nowhere else in the world.Some iconic endemic species include mountain goat,lion tailed macaque.and several species of frogs snakes and plants,
